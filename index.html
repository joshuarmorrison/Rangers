U.S. Army Rangers

Army Rangers work 95% of the time at night. While you sleep, they are taking the fight to the enemy.

Army Rangers are America's elite direct action raid force. Rangers are a rapid deployment capable small unit. Able to deployed anywhere in the world in less than 18 hours mission ready upon arrival. Ranger regiments operate on a rotation system with each other.
Rangers operate generally in 8 man teams.
Rangers occasionally do company sized operations.
Snipers in regiment operate in 3 man teams and are deployed as 3 man teams.
Snipers go through over 36 weeks of extensive and rigorous training.
Rangers train local and national Law Enforcement Agencies.

Ranger Training

- Rangers go through months of rigorous training. The training a ranger goes through varies for every candidate. However every ranger must go through the Ranger Assessment & Selection Program as well as Ranger School.

- Ranger Assessment & Selection is 8 weeks long and tests every Ranger Candidate mentally, physically, and emotionally. RASP for short, is grueling with less than 20% of the candidates being selected for life in Ranger Regiment.

Ranger School is a 62 day course designed to test the mental, physical, and emotional state of each participant. Throughout the grueling 62 days ranger classes lose over 85% of their starting numbers.

Battalion

Ranger Battalion is a very rewarding and coveted unit to belong to. Regiment is a family. The brothers you serve with will always be by your side, they will always be there for you when you need them.

Mindset

A ranger is a person who knows what their mind, body, and emotions are capable of. The intestinal fortitude to arrive on the objective, no matter what that objective is, ready to fight. Willing to lay down their own life so others can live. Being physically fit is only a fraction of what embodies a ranger within regiment.

Regiment Life

Life in regiment is ran by a set of rules that allow you to remain within regiment. Waive from the rules and you will be sent to the Conventional Army. Adhering to the insanely high rules in regiment is expected of every person their regardless of rank.

Ranger Motto

“It is not death that a man should fear, but he should fear never beginning to live.”
